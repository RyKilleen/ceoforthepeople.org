Who We Are

CEO exists to actively disrupt structures of oppression by providing youth and young adults of color with opportunities to cultivate potential, encourage self-empowerment, and enhance their communities through meaningful employment, engagement, and participation. We connect under-represented youth and young adults with the support they need to overcome whatever obstacles they face.

Our Team

Marguarite Gooden

Marguerite is a retired Arlington Public Schools Principal. Native resident of Halls Hill and a community activist!

Matt Rogers

Matt is the Chief of Staff to Loudoun County Chair At-Large Phyllis J. Randall. A native of Philadelphia, PA and alumnus of Virginia Commonwealth University in Richmond, VA, Matt came to the Chair’s office in 2021 after 4.5 consecutive years of working around the Virginia General Assembly – 3.5 of those most recent years as the Chief of Staff to a Virginia State Senator. In 2021, Matt was himself a candidate for the old Virginia House of Delegates’ 47th district in Arlington, VA. In his limited spare time, Matt is the Board President of WERA 96.7 in Arlington, VA and the host of a sports podcast named “Honorable Mention” frequented by famous voices from ESPN, NBC, CBS, Sports Radio 94WIP, and others. He now lives in Loudoun County, VA with his wife, Jasmine, and his son, Mandela.

Quimari Mullins

Quimari is a 25 year old Arlington native whose mission is to be a light and resource in the lives of youth in her community. Currently she teaches Pre-Kindergarten in a private school in Arlington. Growing up in a turbulent environment propelled her into what she hopes will be a lifetime of service.

Zak Mahshie

Zak is General Counsel and co-owner of Excella, Inc., a technology services company bringing innovation to private and public sector clients. Through Excella’s ImpaX program, Zak has been active in supporting organizations targeting the root causes of homelessness.

Zakiya Worthey

Zakiya is the CEO of K. Lamb, a human-centered consulting firm that provides strategic business planning, workplace culture and organizational consulting, diversity, equity, inclusion and belonging competencies, and change management best practices and techniques. She is also active in the community, serving on multiple non-profit boards and is the Commissioner of the NoVA Juvenile Detention Center Commission.
